    • What is instrumental English?

    The teaching/learning of foreign languages ​​as an instrumental discipline has aroused great interest in Brazil in recent decades. Instrumental English is known as English for Specific Purposes (ESP) and its main objective is to enable the student to read and understand academic texts in English, using specific reading strategies and techniques within a scheme of autonomous activities.

    Although the student is the protagonist in this social conception of reading, the teacher also plays a prominent role as a facilitator in this process of joint construction, as it will be up to him to accompany and assist the student until he assumes responsibility for his development, demonstrating that he is able to perform the activities alone.

    Grammar is taught in a contextualized way, helping to understand academic texts. The worked vocabulary is mainly related to the student's field of study.


    What makes the course different?

    We use 2 types of techniques for your understanding: Scaning e Skimming


    What is Scaning?

    It is a form of practical and objective reading, which we practice when we need to find specific information in a text that addresses different subjects. This style of reading is usually practiced through keywords.


    What is Skimming?

    This practice consists of observing the text just to detect the general subject, without worrying about the details. Therefore, we must pay attention to the text layout, understand the introductory and concluding paragraphs, in addition to observing non-verbal elements with images, graphs, tables, etc.
